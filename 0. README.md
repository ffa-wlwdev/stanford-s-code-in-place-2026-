### One Night at the C.G. Museum
A console-based detective thriller and educational game — dedicated to the women who changed the course of a war, in secret.


## The Story (Written & developed by yours truly!)

The night before the Annual Exhibition, enigmatic rogues break into the C.G. Museum to steal artifacts and destroy its records.

Three people are scouting the museum tonight — and one of them is you.

C.G. stands for Code Girls — the over 10,000 women recruited by the U.S. Army and Navy as codebreakers during World War II. Their stories were classified for decades. This museum exists to make sure history doesn't forget them.

Tonight, someone is trying to change that history. You won't let them.


## Choose Your Role

🟢 Detective Meyer Driscoll — Cipher Decryption | Difficulty: Medium
The Enigmatic Rogues left five encrypted messages behind. Decode each one using Caesar Cipher logic, fill in the murder board, and track them down before they escape.

🟡 Curator Helen Aderholt — Historical Trivia | Difficulty: Easy
Five placards in the museum have been tampered with. Restore them by answering multiple choice questions about the real Code Girls — with optional research notes to guide you.

🟣 Hacker PURPLE_CIPHER — Cipher + Trivia, No Hints | Difficulty: Advanced
Breach three randomised system nodes — no hints, no safety net. Solve faster, think sharper. And when it's over... the museum might just have a job for you.


## How It Works
* No punishment for wrong answers — every hint explains the why
* HINT command unlocks a CS trivia challenge with full explanations
* CLUE command reveals the full answer if you're stuck
* SOL command for hint questions
* Murder board updates in real time as the Detective solves each cipher
* Hacker mode is replayable — questions are randomly drawn from a pool each run
* All three roles tell different perspectives of the same case and can be played in any order



## What You'll Learn
* Caesar Cipher cryptography — how encryption and decryption work
* Real history of the Code Girls and key figures like Agnes Meyer Driscoll and Genevieve Grotjan Feinstein
* CS fundamentals woven into hints — Python data types, Karel commands, string operations, and more, as taught in CIP.
